Basic Ray Tracer

This are some images I've rendered using a ray tracer I wrote in ~ 3 weeks combined in the UCSD classes Computer Graphics and Computer Graphics II. Without acceleration structures, the rendering of scene7 took about half an hour. Most other scenes took under a minute. I used FreeImage to export the PNGs and glm for matrix calculations. OpenMP was also used to optimize intersection testing. For shading, we used the Blinn-Phong model




From top, clockwise: Scene testing reflcetions; One thousand spheres; Cornell Box; Stanford Dragon.

Part 2: Direct Lighting

You'll notice on the above images, however, that the ray tracer is quite simplistic; The shadows are pitch black and have hard edges, and light seemingly comes out of these infinitesimally small points (in the case of the Cornell Box). For part two of this project, we implemented a more realistic means of direct lighting, in which we integrate over the area of area lights to calculate shading.
